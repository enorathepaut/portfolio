Overview

What moves you

A few months before moving three of their very distinct businesses to the same campus, BMW Group asked us to help them bring everyone together by creating an employee brand. We created the brand, its manifestation in the new space and ways to shift the business’ culture towards more collaborative and agile ways of working.

Client

BMWgroup

Project team

  • Matthew Shannon — Creative Director
  • Enora Thépaut — Designer, Art Director
  • Brad Sussex — Designer, Art Director

Description

The brand we created is bold, disruptive and interactive. It seeks to create casual collisions of ideas, of people, of cultures within the workspace. It was applied to spaces in BMW Group's new offices and to their internal communications. But a visual identity by itself can't change a work culture so we created "interventions" to demonstrate the new values.

Description

With the first intervention we wanted to show people within BMW Group that they could drop their work persona and be autentically themselves in the work place. By asking them a very personal and surprising question we were able to reveal and share their true personalities.

“Some fantastic creative ideas delivering tangible outcomes which push the boundaries of engagement beyond what we have done before. I'm keeping a close-eye on this work to roll-out globally.” BMW Group Board Member

Description

For the second intervention, BMW Group employees were greeted one monday morning with a bag of chalk on their desk and a question painted in large letters on the wall: "If you could do anything here what would it be?". The answers over the next week ranged from funny to sarcastic to really innovative. The best ideas were then adopted by the business.

“I had the best time this afternoon!! Loved being a painter and decorator! People were cracking up and asking questions and it sent the whole office into a frenzy.
People are saying that they cannot wait to see what's next.” Jodie, What Moves You ambassador

Description

The third intervention aimed to show the employee's personalities. We set up photobooths in each location where we asked people to express an emotion as their portrait was taken. The result was a vibrant series of portraits that was used on the employees' personal profiles instead of their old corporate shots.

“I Just wanted to say a huge well done to everyone! Loved the pics this afternoon, made my week!” Lee, Change Manager at BMW Group

Description

Collaboration was the key for the fourth intervention, we gave each employee a numbered piece of scalectrics and a mission to complete the giant scalectrics track. Some pieces had been dispatched in the wrong locations, they had to be exchanged before the racing could start.

“Oh My Goodness—I knew you could do it!!! Creative genius! Love! Love! Love! The new last intervention!” Sarah, BMW Group client lead

All four interventions were then summarised in a short film which helped employees make sense of the new brand and its values. The values and the brand are now theirs to develop and live by.
